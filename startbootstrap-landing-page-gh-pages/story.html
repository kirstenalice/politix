Our Story

Our first voting experience did not go smoothly.

We were sitting in a cafe on campus frantically trying to figure out which Hoboken Mayoral candidate we each wanted to vote for. There were varying levels of information in different places online and it was hard to understand one candidate’s whole platform. Each time we wanted to know a certain candidates position on a certain topic we would have to go back to Google. We had to sift through increasingly more and more search results just to find out if Michael DeFusco supported the legalization of marijuana.

Two dipshit college freshmen awkwardly walked into our district building to vote for Governor of New Jersey and Mayor of Hoboken. Neither of us were actually correctly registered to vote so we got put in a separate room where “mail-in”s voted. There were seven positions open and votable, we only knew two names on the ballot.

It didn’t feel right submitting an uninformed vote, and we can’t imagine we were the only young people voting for the first time that felt this way. If we had one place we could look for all the people running for government, we would have been fully comfortable voting for the person we believed right for us. Also, we had no idea that there were other positions we needed to vote for other than Governor and Mayor. If we had known this before, we could have also made informed decisions on the rest of the ballot.

People want their information fast and all in one place. We felt majorly inconvenienced having to do in depth searching to find out who was running for Hoboken mayor, let alone if we had known to search for the other candidates for the other positions.

Politix can provide that information fast and all in one place. With the help of Politix, no one has to be an uninformed voter again. Politix will make sure the people elect the kind of people they really want in office.
